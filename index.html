üåæ About the University

The Tamil Nadu Agricultural University (TNAU) came into existence in 1871. It is one of the best State Agricultural Universities (SAU) in India, ranked 6th in Agriculture and Allied Sector Category by NIRF (2025). Internationally, TNAU ranked 105 in QS-BRICS and is the first SAU accredited by the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR). The University has received prestigious recognition as the best institution and best performing university by ICAR.

Currently, TNAU offers 13 Undergraduate degree programmes, Master‚Äôs and Doctoral programmes in 12 disciplines, and Diploma (Agri.) programmes in 11 centres. Diploma in Agri-inputs and Certificate programmes are also offered through ODL mode. The University has released more than 900 varieties and hybrids of cereals, millets, pulses, horticultural, commercial, fodder and forage crops till 2025.

üî¨ About the Institute

Tamil Nadu Rice Research Institute (TRRI), under TNAU, is located at Aduthurai, Thanjavur district, Tamil Nadu. It has been serving Indian agricultural development for nearly 103 years and is actively engaged in rice and rice-based research and extension.

TRRI functions as the Lead Centre for Rice Research and coordinates Region II of TNAU. The research stations under this region include RRS Virudhachalam, ORS Tindivanam, SRS Cuddalore, SRS Sirugamani, SRS Melathur, RRS Tirur, ARS Virinjipuram, CEM Athiyandal, NPRC CRS Veppanthattai, ARS Thanjavur and ARS Pattukottai.

So far, TRRI has released 60 rice varieties, 1 rice hybrid, 7 black gram varieties, 3 green gram varieties, and one variety each of sunnhemp, cotton and soybean, besides introducing 14 rice varieties.

üåç About the National Conference

Plant genetic resources for food and agriculture form the biological basis of global food security and directly or indirectly support the livelihoods of every person on earth. These include traditional varieties, modern cultivars and other plant species used for food, feed, fibre, clothing, shelter, timber and energy.

These resources are the raw materials for developing new cultivars through conventional breeding or biotechnology. The history of plant genetic resources dates back more than 10,000 years when farmers selected useful traits from wild species. Conservation of endangered plants is vital to avoid loss of valuable genetic traits. Seeds are the most convenient materials for conservation due to ease of storage, transport and multiplication.

‚öñÔ∏è Protection of Plant Varieties and Farmers‚Äô Rights Act, 2001

The Protection of Plant Varieties and Farmers‚Äô Rights (PPV&FR) Act was enacted by the Government of India on October 30, 2001 to protect traditional crop varieties and safeguard the interests of farmers and plant breeders involved in the development of new varieties.

The Protection of Plant Varieties and Farmers‚Äô Rights Authority was established to implement the Act. The main features include protection of crop varieties, establishment of farmers‚Äô rights, preservation of traditional varieties, and prevention of extinction of indigenous genetic resources.

The Act recognizes the multiple roles played by farmers in cultivating, conserving, developing and selecting plant varieties. It acknowledges the value added by farmers to wild species and traditional landraces through selection and identification of economically important traits.
